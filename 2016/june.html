Empire State of Mind New York

I love New York. It's by far the greatest city in the world by a factor of 10. I've been going to New York at least once a year since I was born, often two to three times to visit family. More often than not, I don't do too much other than hang out with Family or do some touristy stuff like going to 5th Ave., Columbus Circle, Little Eately or the Brooklyn Bridge.

This time, I was lucky enough to be shown around by a New Yorker for almost two weeks. Nicole and I hit up some awesome stuff in the two short weeks. Chromeo at Flash Factory; Governors Ball where we saw the legenday Taquerito food truck, m83, Misterwives, De La Sol, The Killers, Miike Snow, and a lot of rain (they cancelled the last day); Sushi Yasuda–touted as one of the best sushi restaurants in all of NYC; Minetta Tavern– the best burger that I've ever had, ever; 90's night at La Poisson rouge; Lobster Boat, which is the coolest idea ever: a free boat that routinely goes up and down the Hudson river as long as you eat Lobster or drink beer; A Rose Cruise with unlimited rose. Needless to say, it was definitely one of the best trips ever.

Gov Ball Rain Out

It may be difficult to see in this photo but it was raining harder than I've ever taken a shower. The downpour was so bad that they cancelled the festival the next day (luckily we were only going on Saturday). We also lucked out that Nicole's friend had been working VIP and let us in to this nice covered area. Otherwise, I would've been in the running for a wet white t-shirt contenst.

Sushi Yasuda

I don't actually have a photo from this Sushi place so I found one off of google. Nicole worked some magic and was able to get us a reservation here at this Sushi restaurant, regarded as one of the best sushi restuarants in all of New York. It was absolutely fucking incredible. I didn't think sushi could make a person so happy.
