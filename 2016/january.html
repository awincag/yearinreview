New Years Eve Los Angeles

Making New Year's plans are always a bitch. So many options to choose from but never really the right one. Luckily, Mike's old brother and his friends decided for us: rent a huge 70's style house in Beverly Hills. It was an epic house stuffed with about 15 of us. On New Year's Eve day, Mike and I headed to a beach bar to grab some beers and tacos. Coincidentally we decided to walk to the beach to watch the sunset, almost completely forgetting that it was the last sunset of 2015. There was about 2,500+ people on the beach or so when we got there. Once the sun set, everyone was cheering and clapping. One of the most surreal feelings of community / collective / fee fee foo foo / whatever you want to call it. It was an epic way to end the year. We ended 2016 the same way.

As for the night party, we celebrated the ball drop at the house and went to the Belasco theatre after. We ended up getting a table and I ended up taking a nap at said table. Ended up staying up until like 7am or something. Hangover got our hangover cure with a recently discovered Egglust. Oh yeah, this is where we decided to have a song of the trip, which you should be hearing right now. We listened to 'Panama' about 35 times or so.

What's Brisket? Dallas

In 2015, I met two of my best frieds Jackie and Tara at a random bar on Memorial Day weekend in SF. I've hung out with them in Austin, SF and various other places but never was able to make it out to their turf, Dallas until now. It was an epic trip where I discover what brisket and queso was. And anytime someone gets to discover a new genre of amazing and beautiful food, I'd call that a success.

Pecan Lodge

My first brisket ever. We hit up Pecan Lodge which is one of the best brisket in all of Dallas. Hangover cure for sure.
Dallas Tower
