Best Vacation Ever Iceland

Best vacation I've ever had. Lambert, Jenna, Emily and Mike said the same thing. Fam (the five of us) around Iceland for 10 days in a Land Rover Discovery exploring as much as we possible could. We ended up driving a total of 1,983 miles. I took close to 3,800 photos. Mike took about 8 hours of footage on the gopro. We drove through two rivers (check out the photo of Mike wading through the river, that was the deeper one), ontop of a glacier (check out the snow photos), 50+ waterfalls, tried rotten shark, stayed on a farm, saw the northern lights in full force TWICE. The list goes on. Every 15 minutes of driving was "holy shit this is the most beautiful thing I've ever seen". You'd drive, say that. Drive 15 more minutes and say the same thing. It was truly incredible country and vacation. If you ever have the opportunity to go around the Iceland, make it happen.

One story from the trip: We were in a small fishing town called Isafjordur. It's in the north western part of the island. It's the largest town in about 500 miles at a population of 1,500. It's larger than all the other towns in the Westfjords combined. To pick up the keys for our Airbnb, we had to meet her husband, who happened to own the local pizzeria. When I picked up the keys, I jokingly asked him ""where we should go for dinner?"", subtly suggesting that we weren't sure if there was a restaurant outside of the pizzeria. He casually recommended a fish restaurant down the road. We walked over to the ""house with the red roof"" at dinner time only to find about 10 houses with red roofs. As we were lost in the courtyard, a small man poked his head through a doorway and asked, ""dinner?"". We said yes and he waived us in. We walked in through the 2ft thick wooden walls into this viking hall. The house looked like it was about 500 years old and was split in half. Half tables and seats. The other half being the kitchen. The waiter admited, ""I don't know what's on the menu yet"". About 15 minutes later, the chef (who was 6'4"" and 250lbs) barged through the door and yelled in Icelandic. The waiter walked over to us after and let us know, ""I now know what's for dinner."" He proceeded to tell us the various fishes that the chef had just caught about 2 hours prior to walking in. The fish we ended up getting was the best fish that we've ever had. ever. It turns out that this restaurant was one of the best restaruants in all of Iceland. It was absolutely incredible.
